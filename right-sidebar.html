Famous Fighter

3D Asteroids, built with a physics engine.

Due to a desire to build a more interesting Asteroids game and simulataneously learn the Famo.us front-end JS framework, I decided to jump headfirst into the 3D browser universe and create a 3D ship to float in space.

Why 3D?

Asteroids is a common game to make for new programmers, since the rules are relatively straightforward, and it's fun. It also shows good fundamentals with JavaScript and CSS. I wanted to take the opportunity to show that my skills have expanded far beyond the early beginnings and that Asteroids can be taken to another level. Plus, flying in space is awesome.

Why Famo.us?

Famo.us presented their new framework to the world at the jQuerySF conference in June, and I happened to be an attendee. I quickly realized that there is a wide variety of frameworks available to use on the front-end, and as a software engineer I never know when skills in one framework will be desired. Additionally, rendering in 3D has historically been a challenge for browsers, and Famo.us has committed to creating the best 3D and Physics engine in the industry. Their engine is incredibly performant, calculating all the physics calculations very quickly and rendering graphics at 60 FPS. I knew that this framework was incredibly powerful, that I could harness all their hard work to make an incredible user experience.
